Storyteller — Epilogue

When I look back today, I see a child in a wide compound, listening to stories by candlelight. I see a girl formed by proverbs, songs, and rules woven together in fear and love. I see a teenager crossing thresholds, carrying the quiet weight of rites and the louder call of faith. And I see an adult, sometimes split between old and new worlds, braiding them into something that can hold her.

Culture shaped me. It taught me discipline, respect, belonging… (full text).

The child and culture are never apart. The child becomes an adult. The adult becomes a storyteller. And the story never ends.
